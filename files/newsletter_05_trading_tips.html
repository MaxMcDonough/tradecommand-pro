5 Rules That Separate
Winners from Losers

By Max McDonough · 4 min read

I've watched hundreds of traders blow up their accounts. Not because they couldn't read a chart — but because they couldn't manage themselves. Here are the 5 rules I wish someone had tattooed on my arm when I started.

Rule #1

Never Risk More Than 2% on a Single Trade

This is non-negotiable. If you have a $10,000 account, your max loss on any single trade is $200. Period. This means you can be wrong 10 times in a row and still have 80% of your capital. Most beginners risk 10-20% and one bad week wipes them out.

TradeCommand Pro tip: Our position sizing calculator does this math automatically. Enter your account size and stop-loss distance — we tell you exactly how many shares to buy.
Rule #2

Cut Losers Fast, Let Winners Run

The average retail trader does the exact opposite: they hold losers hoping for a recovery and sell winners early to "lock in gains." This is a psychological trap called loss aversion, and it will bleed your account dry.

TradeCommand Pro tip: Set your stop-loss BEFORE you enter the trade. Our dashboard enforces this — you can't submit a signal without defining your exit.
Rule #3

Trade the Setup, Not the Emotion

FOMO, revenge trading, overconfidence after a win streak — emotions are the #1 account killer. Every trade should be a cold, calculated decision based on your playbook. If there's no setup, there's no trade.

TradeCommand Pro tip: Our AI signals only fire when 3+ technical indicators align. No signal = no trade. It removes the emotional guesswork entirely.
Rule #4

Journal Every Single Trade

If you're not tracking your trades, you're gambling. Write down: why you entered, what the setup was, what your stop was, and what happened. After 50 trades, patterns emerge. You'll see which setups work for you and which don't.

TradeCommand Pro tip: The Activity tab in your CRM panel auto-logs every action. Add notes to any trade with timestamps — your journal builds itself.
Rule #5

Master One Strategy Before Adding Another

Beginners try 10 strategies in 10 days and wonder why nothing works. Pick ONE setup — a breakout play, a mean reversion trade, whatever resonates — and execute it 100 times. Get consistent before you diversify your approach.
