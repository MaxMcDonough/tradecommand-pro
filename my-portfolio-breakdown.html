Glossary — What These Words Mean

Entry Price (You Paid)
The price per share when you bought the stock. If you bought 5 shares of SMCI at $22.23, that's your entry price.
Current Price (Price Now)
What one share is selling for right now on the market.
Total Cost
Shares you own × the price you paid. Example: 5 shares × $22.23 = $111.15. That's the money you put into that stock.
Value Now
Shares you own × today's price. Example: 5 shares × $24.05 = $120.25. That's what your shares are worth right now.
Gain/Loss Per Share
Current price minus what you paid. SMCI: $24.05 - $22.23 = +$1.82 per share. You made $1.82 on every share you hold.
Gain/Loss %
How much you're up or down as a percentage. +8.19% means for every $100 you put in, you've made $8.19.
Stop Loss
A safety net price. If the stock drops to this price, you sell automatically to limit your losses. Think of it like a "bail out" point.
Take Profit (Target)
Your goal price. If the stock rises to this price, you sell and lock in your win.
Cash Available
Money in your account that isn't invested in any stock yet. You can use it to buy more stocks.
Portfolio Value
Everything combined — the value of all your stocks plus your cash. This is your total account balance.
P&L (Profit and Loss)
The difference between what you have now and what you started with. +$24.40 means you've made $24.40 so far.
